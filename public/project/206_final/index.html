D. How have you explored and confirmed that the assumptions of normality and homoscedasticity are satisfied?

Most residuals are evenly distribute on the both sides of the red dashed line in the Residual Variances graph, so the homoscedasticity assumption is satisfied. In the Q-Q plot, the residuals are perfectly at one line, thus the residuals are normally distributed. In Residuals vs Leverage graph, no points are labeled or out of the Cook’s Distance (the red dashed line), therefore, no outliers influenced the model. Graphs mentioned above are only included in the R markdown documents.

E. Adjusted R\(^2\) and its meaning for this model.

In models with multiple predictor variables, the adjusted R\(^2\) could identify whether adding new variables will significantly improve the model, which means the model (1) with the highest adjusted R\(^2\) is the most accurate model comparing to others in our case.

F. Interpret the coefficient value in 1 sentence each in the model.

Body mass(penguin species, sex, flipper length) = -365.82 + -87.63* (Chinstrap penguin) + 836.26 * (Gentoo penguin) +530.38 * (MALE) + 20.02 * (Flipper length)

  • -87.63*(Chinstrap penguin): If everything else is held constant, we would expect a Chinstrap penguin to weigh 87.63 grams less than an Adelie Penguin.

  • 836.26*(Gentoo penguin): If everything else is held constant, we would expect a Gentoo penguin to weigh 836.26 grams more than an Adelie penguin.

  • 530.38*(MALE): If everything else is held constant, we would expect a male penguin to weigh 530.38 grams more than a female penguin.

  • 20.02*(Flipper length): If everything else is held constant, we would expect a 20.02 grams increase for each one millimeter increase in penguin’s flipper length.

B. Is there a statistical difference in the proportion of babies born at low birth weights for smoking vs. non-smoking mothers?

Only a small proportion of babies were reported as low birth weight both among the smoked mothers (n = 18 (14%)) and not smoked mother (n = 92 (11%)). The low birth-weight rates showed no significant difference between groups of mothers who whether smoked during the pregnancy or not according to the Chi-Square test (\(\chi\)2(1) = 1.22, p (0.27) > 0.05), meaning the mothers’ smoking situation and babies’ low birth-weight are independent.

C. The limitation of recording “smoked during pregnancy” as a binary variable.

When using the binary standard to evaluate the smoking influence, we oversimplified the smoking pattern by ignoring many other factors involving smoke. Apparently, only smoking one cigarette one time at the first week of pregnancy is less likely to affect the babies weight than smoking several packs of cigarette everyday during the pregnancy. In other words, we should consider details like the smoking frequency, timing, and amount and their impacts on new babies’ weights.

Major observations from the graph:

  • None of the urchin sizes at five sites followed the normal distribution perfectly with several peaks (3, 4, 5, 6 cm) in size.

  • Though all the purple urchins’ size in different sites fell in 2-8 cm, the urchins from Mohawk (MOHK) had higher average size compared to other groups.

C. Using observations from all years, is there a significant difference in mean purple urchin size in control treatments at the 5 sites?

In control treatment plots, the mean sizes of Purple Urchin (cm) measured in Arroyo Quemado (AQUE) area (4.57 \(\pm\) 1.04 cm, n = 3185)，in Carpinteria (CARP) area (4.17 \(\pm\) 1.13 cm, n = 3430), in Isla Vista (IVEE) area (4.25 \(\pm\) 0.94 cm, n = 1601), in Mohawk (MOHK) area (5.28 \(\pm\) 1.14 cm, n = 3510), and in Naples (NAPL) area (4.05 \(\pm\) 1.11 cm, n = 3219) have significant difference in urchins’ size between at least two sites among these areas by a one-way ANOVA (F(4, 14940) = 692.99, p < 0.001). Density ridgeline graph presented in Figure.3.

To further study which groups differ significantly with each other, we analyzed with Tukey’s test for Honestly Significant Differences (Tukey’s HSD). The results showed that while there is no significant difference in the mean purple urchins size between Isla Vista (IVEE) and Carpinteria (CARP) area (p adj(0.098) > 0.05), other areas are all different significantly among each other (p adj < 0.001).
